Over a short few weeks, my team and I sourced raw data, performed a significant analysis, and presented the results in an interactive and user-friendly way.


The Problem

The problem was stated that typical metrics used to assess NFL quarterbacks didn't account for the player's ability to make the best decisions during a given play. We set out to explore the possibility of isolating the moment that a quarterback chose to pass, and evaluate the field at that time. From there, we could assess whether or not the pass was to the best player available.

The Data

Was sourced from the NFL. We received text files of player tracking data. These separate files included various sensor readings of all players on the field throughout some 2017 games. We loaded the data into a PostgreSQL database on AWS, and began our analysis.

The Analysis

In order to evaluate the quality of a decision, we created a value metric based on the receiver. Every eligible receiver has a value that changes based on their field position as well as other factors. What we had to do next was train a model to determine the probability of a given receiver catching a pass and actually realizing his estimated value. Significant feature engineering was needed to build the catch probability model. It included several factors like distance to the quarterback and distance to defenders. One interesting approach we took was to vectorize the relative paths of the receivers (dx,dy), and use KMeans to classify the route. Finally, after testing several models, we trained a Random Forest classifier to return the catch probability (RF gave similar results to XGBoost but was faster to train). With our catch probabilities, we could weight the value metric to give us "Expected Yards Added"

The Presentation

Knowing that the potential audience might not be interested in the math, we developed a simple web-based dashboard experience for exploring the 2017 quarterback lineup based on the player's ability to select the receiver with the highest EYA at the time he passed the ball. That's QBViz! Enjoy!
